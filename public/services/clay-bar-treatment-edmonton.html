Clay Bar Process

Step 1: Wash & Pre-Clean

Vehicles receive a thorough wash and iron remover to eliminate loose contaminants before claying begins.

Step 2: Clay Application

We glide clay across lubricated surfaces, folding frequently to expose clean material and avoid scratching.

Step 3: Inspection

Panels are inspected by touch and light to confirm a smooth finish. Spot claying addresses missed areas.

Step 4: Protection

A sealant, wax, or ceramic topper is applied to protect freshly decontaminated paint.

Step 5: Final Detail

Tires dressed, glass cleaned, and trim wiped to complete the exterior transformation.

Clay Bar FAQs

How often should I clay bar my car?

Most vehicles benefit from clay treatment every 6–12 months or whenever the paint feels rough after washing.

Will clay remove scratches?

No. Clay removes contamination but not scratches. Pair it with paint correction for defect removal.

Is clay safe for ceramic coatings?

Yes, with the correct clay media and lubricant. We also offer coating-safe decontamination options.
