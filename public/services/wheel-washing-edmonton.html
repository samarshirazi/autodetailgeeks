Wheel Detail Process

Step 1: Pre-Rinse & Degrease

High-pressure rinse removes loose debris, then iron remover breaks down brake dust.

Step 2: Brush & Clean

Wheel faces, barrels, lug nuts, and calipers are brushed individually to ensure every surface is spotless.

Step 3: Tire Scrub

Old tire dressing and grime are stripped away before applying fresh conditioner.

Step 4: Dry & Protect

Compressed air and microfiber towels dry wheels before applying sealant or ceramic coating.

Step 5: Final Inspection

We wipe wheel wells, touch up brake calipers, and ensure tires have an even, satin finish.

Wheel Detailing FAQs

How often should wheels be detailed?

We recommend monthly cleaning for daily drivers and more frequent care in winter to remove salt.

Do you remove wheels for detailing?

We clean wheels thoroughly on-vehicle. For coatings or show vehicles, we offer wheel-off detailing by request.

Will cleaners damage my brakes?

No. Our products are safe for brake components and we rinse thoroughly to remove any residue.
