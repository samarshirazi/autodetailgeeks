Our Ceramic Coating Process

Step 1: Inspection & Consultation

We evaluate paint condition, previous protection, and your driving habits. From there we recommend the best coating package and outline the preparation required.

Step 2: Decontamination Wash

A multi-stage wash, iron remover, and clay bar treatment strip away road film, rail dust, and embedded contaminants to deliver a surgically clean surface.

Step 3: Paint Correction

Machine polishing removes swirl marks, holograms, and light scratches while restoring clarity. Correcting defects before coating ensures the gloss you see now lasts for years.

Step 4: Panel Preparation

We use dedicated surface prep solutions to remove polishing oils so the ceramic coating can chemically bond to the clear coat without interference.

Step 5: Precise Coating Application

The ceramic coating is applied panel by panel, leveled, and inspected under high-CRI lighting. Wheels, glass, and trim can be coated as part of upgrade packages.

Step 6: Curing & Aftercare

We allow proper flash and cure times, then provide you with maintenance instructions, wash kits, and optional monthly checkups for lasting performance.

Built for Edmonton Weather

Winter road salt, gravely spring slush, and summer sun are constant threats to unprotected paint. Ceramic coatings create a sacrificial layer that resists corrosion, road film, and UV fade. The hydrophobic finish sheds water and prevents dirt from clinging, keeping your vehicle cleaner between washes.

Because ceramic coatings harden above your clear coat, they also protect against micro-scratches from brushes and snow removal. That means better gloss retention, easier maintenance, and higher resale value—critical advantages for Edmonton vehicles exposed to extreme seasonal shifts.

Ceramic Coating FAQs

How long does ceramic coating last?

Our professional coatings last 3 to 7 years depending on the package. Routine hand washing with pH-neutral soap and periodic inspections help the coating reach its full lifespan.

Is ceramic coating better than wax?

Yes. Ceramic coating bonds to the clear coat and forms a much harder, more durable layer than wax. It resists chemicals, UV, and abrasion far longer, reducing the need for frequent waxing.

Can you coat brand new vehicles?

Absolutely. New vehicles benefit the most, but we still perform light correction to remove factory transport defects before sealing in the finish with ceramic protection.
