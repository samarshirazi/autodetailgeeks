Waxing Process

Step 1: Wash & Decontaminate

Foam wash, iron remover, and clay bar clear away embedded contaminants for a smooth surface.

Step 2: Paint Inspection

We identify areas needing polishing or correction to maximize gloss before sealing in the finish.

Step 3: Wax or Sealant Application

Hand or machine application ensures even coverage. Cure times vary depending on product selection.

Step 4: Buff & Finish

Microfiber buffing reveals a glassy surface. Tires, trim, and glass receive finishing touches.

Step 5: Aftercare Guidance

We provide washing tips and product recommendations to maintain protection between visits.

Waxing FAQs

How often should I wax my car?

We recommend waxing every 3–4 months or seasonally. Sealants and ceramic toppers extend intervals up to 6 months.

Will waxing remove scratches?

Wax fills micro-scratches temporarily. For permanent correction, pair waxing with our paint correction service.

Can you wax matte or satin finishes?

Yes. We use matte-safe sealants that protect without adding unwanted gloss. Let us know your finish type when booking.
