Interior Detailing Workflow

Step 1: Declutter & Vacuum

Personal items are gathered, garbage removed, and high-powered vacuums reach deep into seat tracks and crevices.

Step 2: Steam & Shampoo

Steam loosens grime and disinfects surfaces before hot water extraction lifts stains from carpets and upholstery.

Step 3: Surface Restoration

We clean and condition plastics, leather, and vinyl with material-specific products that leave a satin, non-greasy finish.

Step 4: Glass & Finishing Touches

Crystal-clear glass, polished displays, and scent options finalize the cabin. Protective coatings are applied if selected.

Step 5: Final Walkthrough

We review the completed detail with you, share maintenance tips, and plan future appointments around your schedule.

Interior Detailing FAQs

How long does interior detailing take?

Most interiors take 2–4 hours depending on size and soil level. Minivans and heavily soiled vehicles can take longer—ask for a custom estimate.

Will it remove pet hair and odors?

Yes. We use specialized pet hair tools, steam, and enzyme neutralizers to remove smells and allergens from fabrics.

How soon can I drive after detailing?

You can drive right away. Seats may remain slightly damp for a few hours after deep shampooing; we provide protective covers as needed.
