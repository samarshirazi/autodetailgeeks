Hand Wash Process

Step 1: Foam Pre-Soak

Thick foam breaks down dirt and lubricates the surface before any contact washing.

Step 2: Two-Bucket Wash

Separate wash and rinse buckets with grit guards keep mitts clean while we wash top-down.

Step 3: Wheel & Tire Cleaning

Dedicated tools remove brake dust and restore tire sidewalls before dressing.

Step 4: Dry & Protect

Soft microfiber towels and forced air prevent water spots; optional sealants add gloss.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Door jambs, trim, and glass are cleaned; final inspection ensures a spotless finish.

Hand Wash FAQs

How often should I hand wash my vehicle?

Weekly or bi-weekly washes keep contaminants from bonding and preserve protective coatings.

Is a hand wash safe for matte paint?

Yes. We use matte-safe soaps and drying towels to keep finishes uniform without gloss.

Do you offer memberships?

Yes. Ask about wash packages and memberships that guarantee priority scheduling and savings.
