Engine Detailing Workflow

Step 1: Engine Cool-Down

We confirm the engine is cool to the touch to prevent thermal shock and protect components.

Step 2: Cover & Protect

Air intakes, alternators, and exposed wiring are shielded before cleaning begins.

Step 3: Clean & Degrease

Foam, steam, and brushes break down grime, followed by careful rinsing and wipe-down.

Step 4: Dry & Inspect

We remove moisture with air compressors, then inspect the bay for leaks or areas needing attention.

Step 5: Dress & Protect

Plastics, hoses, and metal surfaces are dressed for a clean, factory finish that resists dust and UV damage.

Engine Detailing FAQs

Is engine detailing safe?

Yes. We use controlled techniques and protect sensitive components to ensure your engine runs perfectly afterward.

How often should I clean my engine bay?

We recommend once or twice a year depending on driving conditions. Vehicles used off-road or in winter benefit from more frequent cleaning.

Will detailing help me find leaks?

Absolutely. A clean engine bay makes it easier to identify fluid leaks and worn components during maintenance.
